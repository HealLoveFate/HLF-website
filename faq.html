Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Everything you need to know about our sessions, practices, and community.

What happens in a healing circle?
Step into sacred circles guided by experienced practitioners. Intentional community, shared ritual, and honest conversation open pathways to profound healing — each session held with care and intention.
How do love and self-discovery practices work?
Immersive sessions explore the inner work that shapes our relationships. Somatic healing, attachment patterns, and self-compassion practices come together to create new possibilities for love and connection in your life.
Are there workshops and certification programs?
Yes — hands-on workshops are led by experienced guides across healing fundamentals, somatic and breathwork practices, and small-group integration sessions. Certification programs are available for those deepening their commitment to the healing path.
How do I connect with the community?
Our community spaces are designed for genuine belonging. Sessions, gatherings, and circles bring together seekers, guides, and healers on the same path — connections that often extend well beyond the session itself.
Why does each session feel different?
Each session reflects your current state — physically, emotionally, and energetically. Because you are always shifting, the experience responds accordingly, creating a unique encounter every time.
How is sound healing different from music?
Sound healing focuses on frequency and resonance rather than melody or rhythm. The tones produced by instruments like singing bowls are chosen for their vibrational qualities — working directly with the nervous system to shift your inner state.
How often should I attend sessions?
It depends on your rhythm. Some people benefit from regular weekly or bi-weekly sessions as an ongoing practice, while others come occasionally for a reset or during a period of transition. There is no single right answer — trust what your body and schedule need.
Is it normal to feel emotional, and can this replace medical care?
In a relaxed state, inner emotions may gently surface — this is natural and welcomed as part of the process. That said, sound healing and Reiki are experience-based practices focused on relaxation, awareness, and state alignment. They are not a substitute for medical care or diagnosis. For any health-related concerns, professional medical advice is always recommended.
